**Athlete base:** ~6 km/week · **Days:** 3/week · **Goal:** finish 21.1 km
**Plan length:** 20 weeks · **Start:** 8 Jun 2026

> Built on three rules: (1) most running **easy/conversational**, (2) weekly volume
> grows **≤10%** with a cutback every ~4th week, (3) the **long run** is the engine.

## Weekly structure (3 runs)
- **E — Easy:** conversational pace, could talk in full sentences. Most of your running.
- **Q — Quality:** short, slightly harder. Early weeks = easy + strides; later = intervals/tempo.
- **L — Long:** slow and steady, the key session. Walk breaks are fine early on.

## How this adapts
Once your watch data flows, I'll calibrate:
- **Easy pace** from your actual HR (target easy = roughly 70–80% of max HR)
- Whether to **hold a week** if a jump felt too hard (resting HR / fatigue signals)
- **10km race-pace target** after Week 10 steady runs
- **Half marathon goal pace** based on your 10km result

If you miss a week (travel/illness), don't cram — repeat the last completed week and
continue. Consistency beats any single workout.
